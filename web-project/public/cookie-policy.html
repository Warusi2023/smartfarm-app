Cookie Policy

How we use cookies and tracking technologies

Last Updated: January 2024

1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how SmartFarm ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website or use our mobile applications (collectively, the "Service").

This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy, which provides additional information about how we collect, use, and protect your personal information.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies allow a website to recognize your device and store some information about your preferences or past actions.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the Service to function properly. They enable core functionality such as:

  • User authentication and login
  • Session management
  • Security features
  • Load balancing

These cookies cannot be disabled as they are essential for the Service to work.

3.2 Functional Cookies

These cookies enhance functionality and personalization, including:

  • Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Language preferences
  • Theme preferences (dark/light mode)
  • User interface customization

3.3 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our Service by collecting and reporting information anonymously. They track:

  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Features used
  • Error messages encountered
  • Performance metrics

3.4 Marketing Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver relevant advertisements and track campaign effectiveness. They may be set by our advertising partners.

5. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set their own cookies, including:

  • Google Analytics: For website analytics and usage statistics
  • Google Maps: For location services and mapping features
  • Weather Services: For weather data and forecasts
  • Payment Processors: For secure payment processing

These third parties have their own privacy policies and cookie practices. We encourage you to review their policies.

6. Local Storage and Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other similar technologies:

  • Local Storage: Stores data locally in your browser for faster access
  • Session Storage: Stores temporary data for the current session
  • IndexedDB: For storing larger amounts of structured data
  • Web Beacons: Small images used to track email opens and page views

These technologies serve similar purposes to cookies and are subject to the same privacy protections.

7. Managing Cookies

7.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Delete existing cookies
  • Set preferences for specific websites

Note: Blocking essential cookies may prevent the Service from functioning properly.

7.2 Cookie Consent

When you first visit our Service, we will ask for your consent to use non-essential cookies. You can:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Customize your cookie preferences
  • Change your preferences at any time through settings

7.3 Mobile App Settings

For mobile applications, you can manage tracking preferences through:

  • App settings within the application
  • Device settings (iOS Settings, Android Settings)
  • Platform-level privacy controls

8. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals websites you visit that you do not want to have your online activity tracked. Currently, there is no standard for how DNT signals should be interpreted.

We do not currently respond to DNT signals. However, you can control tracking through your cookie preferences and browser settings.

9. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you choose to disable cookies, some features of the Service may not function properly:

  • You may need to log in more frequently
  • Your preferences may not be saved
  • Some features may be unavailable
  • Performance may be affected

10. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons.

We will notify you of any material changes by:

  • Posting the updated policy on our website
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date
  • Sending email notifications for significant changes
